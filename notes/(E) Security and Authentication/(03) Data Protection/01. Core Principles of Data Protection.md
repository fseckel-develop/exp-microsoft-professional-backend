
Protecting data requires ensuring it remains private, accurate, and accessible. The foundation of data protection is built on three core principles: confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA).

---
## Core Principles

### Confidentiality

- Limits access to sensitive information
	- Only authorized users or systems can view data
- Prevents:
	- Unauthorized access
	- Disclosure
	- Misuse of sensitive information
- Common mechanisms:
	- Encryption
	- Secure authentication
	- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Analogy:
	- Like a phone lock screen that only the owner can unlock
- Example:
	- Restricting customer data access to specific employees

### Integrity

- Ensures data remains accurate and consistent
	- Throughout its entire lifecycle
- Protects against:
	- Accidental changes
	- Intentional tampering
	- Data corruption
- Common mechanisms:
	- Validation checks
	- Audits
	- Backups
- Analogy:
	- A text message arriving exactly as it was sent
- Example:
	- Verifying order and inventory records remain correct

### Availability

- Ensures data and systems are accessible when needed
	- Only for authorized users
- Focuses on:
	- Minimizing downtime
	- Maintaining productivity
	- Ensuring timely access to critical information
- Common mechanisms:
	- Redundant systems
	- Regular maintenance
	- Disaster recovery planning
- Analogy:
	- A library that is always open
- Example:
	- Hosting services on reliable infrastructure with failover support

---
## Common Threats to Data Protection

Each threat targets one or more CIA principles and can undermine trust in systems and data.

### Unauthorised Access

- Occurs when users gain access without permission
- Impacts:
	- Confidentiality
- Consequences:
	- Exposure of private or sensitive information
- Example:
	- Hackers accessing patient medical records

### Data Corruption or Alteration

- Data is modified accidentally or maliciously
- Impacts:
	- Integrity
- Consequences:
	- Inaccurate records
	- Poor decision-making
	- Loss of credibility
- Example:
	- Altered financial records producing false reports

### Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks

- Systems are overwhelmed with excessive requests
- Impacts:
	- Availability
- Consequences:
	- Legitimate users cannot access services
	- Operational downtime
- Example:
	- An online store crashing during peak traffic

#### Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS)

- A coordinated DoS attack from multiple sources
- Characteristics:
	- Traffic originates from many compromised devices
	- Harder to detect and block
- Example:
	- Botnets flooding a website during a major sale

---
## Integrated Data Protection Strategy

- Effective protection requires applying all three principles together
	- Confidentiality:
		- Access restrictions and encryption
	- Integrity:
		- Validation, monitoring, and backups
	- Availability:
		- Redundancy and disaster recovery
- Combined result:
	- Secure
	- Reliable
	- Trustworthy systems

---
## Key Takeaways

- Confidentiality protects privacy
- Integrity ensures accuracy and trust
- Availability guarantees access
- Understanding threats helps design stronger defenses
- The CIA triad forms the foundation of modern data protection
