## Core Idea of Scalability

- Scalability enables modern applications to support growing demand while remaining reliable and efficient  
- It prepares systems to expand smoothly without degrading user experience  
- Closely connected to performance, requiring careful balance and planning  

---
## Key Scalability Principles

- **Horizontal Scaling**
	- Adds more servers or nodes to distribute workload evenly  
	- Improves capacity by spreading user traffic across multiple machines  
	- Commonly used to handle traffic spikes and large user bases  

- **Vertical Scaling**
	- Increases the power of a single server  
	- Achieved by upgrading CPU, RAM, or storage  
	- Useful for handling more intensive workloads on one machine  

- **Elasticity**
	- Automatically scales resources up or down based on real-time demand  
	- Optimises cost efficiency and resource utilisation  
	- Reduces the need for manual scaling decisions  

- **Availability**
	- Ensures the system remains accessible at all times  
	- Maintains uptime during peak demand and off-hours  
	- Critical for meeting user expectations  

- **Fault Tolerance**
	- Allows the system to continue operating despite component failures  
	- Prevents single points of failure from causing outages  
	- Ensures graceful handling of errors and disruptions  

---
## Scalability and Performance Relationship

- **Scalability**
	- Focuses on handling increased workloads by adding resources  
	- Supports long-term growth and adaptability  

- **Performance**
	- Measures how fast and efficiently a system responds under current load  
	- Directly impacts user experience and satisfaction  

- **Trade-offs**
	- Scaling can increase costs if not managed carefully  
	- Performance optimisations may limit scalability without proper design  
	- Balance is required to achieve growth without sacrificing responsiveness  

---
## How the Principles Work Together

- Horizontal scaling handles user traffic surges  
- Vertical scaling strengthens critical components like databases  
- Elasticity adapts capacity during peak and low usage periods  
- Availability guarantees uninterrupted access  
- Fault tolerance keeps the system running even during failures  

---
## Conclusion

- Scalability enables systems to grow with demand  
- Performance ensures systems remain fast and responsive  
- Together, they form the foundation of reliable, future-proof system design
